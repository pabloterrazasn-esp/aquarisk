# Spain Premium Spec v1

## Strategic role

Spain is the first premium deployment of AquaRisk and TerraNava's reputational anchor for European cooperation. It must exceed the baseline architecture used elsewhere.

## Why Spain is premium

- higher expectation of cartographic rigor
- higher expectation of official-source traceability
- stronger scrutiny from technical and public-sector interlocutors
- direct reputational value for European funding and cooperation

## Target standard

Spain premium should visibly outperform the baseline in:

- DEM quality
- station traceability
- hydrography quality
- report clarity
- methodological disclosure
- country-level coverage documentation

## Mandatory components

- national DEM backbone documented and justified
- hillshade, slope, and relief profile products
- hydrography and basin hierarchy aligned with official or premium-grade references when beneficial
- traced station catalog with operator, status, confidence, and verification date
- country methodology note
- example point report
- example basin report
- coverage panel visible in AquaRisk
- public disclaimer separating support to decision from official service

## Source policy

Preferred order:

1. official Spanish or national geospatial/hydrological sources
2. European reference products
3. open fallback datasets with TerraNava traceability

## Public positioning

Spain should be described as:

- `caso premium en preparacion` until checklist is complete
- `despliegue premium` only after country activation QA passes

## Deliverables for Phase 1

- Spain listed in coverage matrix
- premium roadmap published internally
- source inventory prepared
- service-level target fixed at:
  - L1-L2 during preparation
  - L3 minimum at first public activation
